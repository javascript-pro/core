> It was a dark and stormy night…

Charles Schulz knew this and used it as a comic device: Snoopy imagines himself a grand novelist but always begins with the most notoriously mocked opener in English literature. The humour comes from the dog’s earnest seriousness contrasted with his terrible taste in prose.

- It’s a running gag in Peanuts.
- It’s a nod to Bulwer-Lytton’s melodramatic style.
- It works as a joke about writerly clichés and our delusion of literary grandeur
