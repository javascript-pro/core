> White seabream – Diplodus sargus sargus

Fish with oval, very tall and compressed body. The colouration is silvery grey, darker on the snout. Has 8–9 alternating light and dark stripes on the flanks. The rear edge of the caudal is black. Forms small groups that remain near the seabed. Lives on sandy and rocky seabeds near rocks from 2–3 to more than 50 metres. Measures up to 45 centimetres.
